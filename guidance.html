Benefits Of Sports And Recreation

Sports and Recreation provides us with many health benefits! Regular physical activity reduces the risk of many health issues such as:

  • Heart disease
  • Stroke
  • Reduced blood pressure
  • Prevention of certain cancers
  • Reduced risk of becoming overweight
  • Improved muscle strength, flexibility, and endurance

Physical activity can also provide social benefits such as:

  • Increased confidence
  • Reduced depression and anxiety
  • Improved quality of life

Physical activity also benefits students because active students have a higher capacity to learn and be productive than students who are not physically active.

The communal experiences from belonging to a team in a sport also brings long term and short term benefits like:

  • Learning to trust and depend on others
  • Accepting and giving help
  • Working together towards a common goal

Commiting to a team while doing a fun sport can help you establish an excercise habit.

Training in sports and exercise especially with a coach can help grow a growth mindset because you will know that even if you cannot do something today, you can practice and achieve it eventually!

Learning from failure through sports can bring resilience and self awareness which can help you through all stages of life.

Methods Of Sports And Recreation

Sports and recreation does not have to be just "sports"! It can consist of:

  • Recreational Walking: Walking is a very mundane activity, however it is a great way to excercise
  • Working out at the gym
  • Running
  • Swimming
  • Cycling
  • Yoga

Adventure Sports: If you don't enjoy mainstream sports, you might find these recreational methods fun too:

  • Boating
  • Hiking
  • Skiing

Common sports you might enjoy:

  • Tennis
  • Golf
  • Basketball
  • Soccer/football
  • Table Tennis

Common things keeping you from excercising

  • Not enough time
  • Not enjoying sports and recreational activities
  • Limited budget
  • Little access to transportation
  • Low motivation
  • Health issues
  • Screen time
